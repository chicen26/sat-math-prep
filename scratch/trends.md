# Digital SAT Math — Trends & Question-Writing Guide (target 790–800, Sept 2026)

## Verified structure (confirmed against College Board)
- **Math section:** 2 modules, 35 min each = 70 min, 44 questions total (~22/module). Whole test is 2h14m. Source: https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/sat/whats-on-the-test/structure
- **Adaptive:** Module 1 has mixed difficulty; performance routes you to a harder or easier Module 2. To reach 790–800 you must route into the hard Module 2 AND miss ~0–1 total. Source: https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/sat/whats-on-the-test/structure
- **Format:** ~75% multiple-choice, ~25% student-produced response (grid-in). Built-in **Desmos** graphing calculator allowed on the entire Math section. Source: https://www.princetonreview.com/college-advice/digital-sat-structure
- **Four domains (approx. counts of 44):** Algebra ~35% (13–15), Advanced Math ~35% (13–15), Problem-Solving & Data Analysis ~15% (5–7), Geometry & Trigonometry ~15% (5–7). These are stable, College-Board-published weightings; exact per-form counts vary. Source: https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/practice/content-domains , https://www.piqosity.com/digital-sat-math-test-content/

## High-yield HARD patterns to generate (difficulty 4–5)
1. Nonlinear system (line + parabola/circle) solved for a parameter value that makes them tangent / intersect exactly once (discriminant = 0).
2. Quadratic "number of solutions" problems: solve for the constant so a quadratic has 0, 1, or 2 real solutions via b²−4ac.
3. Exponential growth/decay word problems where the rate is given as a percent per period but the answer needs a different time unit (convert the base/exponent).
4. Function transformation/composition: given f(x), ask for g(x)=af(x−h)+k features (vertex, zeros, range) or f(g(x)) evaluated at a point.
5. Circle equations in general form requiring completing the square to extract center/radius, then a follow-up (point inside? tangent line? diameter endpoints).
6. Right-triangle/unit-circle trig with a twist — sin/cos cofunction identity (sin x = cos(90−x)) or values in radians, plus arc length / sector area.
7. Polynomial behavior: remainder theorem, factor/root relationships, or "for what value of k is (x−a) a factor."
8. Systems of equations with infinitely many or no solutions — solve for the coefficient making lines coincident/parallel.
9. Rational equations producing extraneous solutions, or solving for a variable buried in a denominator.
10. Statistics interpretation: effect on mean vs. median/SD when a value is added/removed, or margin-of-error / confidence-interval reasoning from a survey.
11. Multi-step rate/percent word problems (mixtures, successive percent change, unit conversion) where mis-setup—not arithmetic—is the trap.
12. Linear/quadratic modeling from a table or graph: derive the equation, then extrapolate or solve for an input given an output.

## Recent test-date trends (be explicit on confidence)
- **Confirmed pattern:** Real questions are never released; only topic/trick trends circulate. CONFIRMED themes across recent forms: nonlinear systems, circle equations, exponential growth, trig, complex numbers, rational expressions, percent change, margin of error.
- **Anecdotal (r/SAT + prep blogs, March 14 2026):** Math felt "slightly easier" with multi-step word problems and abstract trig as the only hard spots; easier forms carry a **harsher curve** (−1 ≈ 790–800, −2 ≈ 770–790). Treat as anecdotal. Source: https://ivytutorsnetwork.com/blog/march-14-2026-sat-difficulty-curve-breakdown
- Reported emphasis on **applied/word-problem algebra** and statistics interpretation at the top end — write traps in problem SETUP, not computation. Source: https://prepmaven.com/blog/test-prep/hardest-sat-math-questions/

## Desmos shortcuts top scorers rely on
- Graph two equations; click the **intersection point** for exact solutions (kills any system instantly).
- Type quadratics/circles raw — Desmos auto-shows vertex, intercepts, center; no rearranging needed.
- **Sliders** (type an undefined letter) to solve "for what value of k…" by dragging until a condition is met.
- Table + **regression** for line/curve-of-best-fit and data-modeling questions.
- Practice keyboard entry to save 30–90s/question. Source: https://prepmaven.com/blog/test-prep/desmos-on-the-digital-sat/ , https://bwseducationconsulting.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/Desmos_SAT_Tips_and_Tricks.pdf
